NSF Seed Fund

Using service design and lean methods to attract more diverse, highly-qualified technology innovators to apply for grants

Overview

The National Science Foundation wanted to attract more applicants for their SBIR / STTR grants. I led the 6-week discovery research and uncovered pain points that consequently filtered out qualified applicants. Moreover, potential applicants had negative perceptions about SBIR / STTR grants.

The redesigned site repositioned the grant as “seed funding” and boasts a clearer, founder-friendly value proposition.

Service design

In collaboration with the stakeholders, we assembled journey maps and painstakingly stitched together a comprehensive service blueprint of the end-to-end application journey. These artifacts allowed us to identify pain points and systemic problems within their processes. The findings helped inform priorities, including delivery of a more simplified, transparent timeline-based application process on the site.
